Curry

Descriptions

Curry is a variety of dishes originating in the Indian subcontinent that use a complex combination of spices or herbs, usually including ground turmeric, cumin, coriander, ginger, and fresh or dried chilies.

In southern India, curry leaves from the curry tree are also an integral ingredient. Turmeric is the main spice in curry, having a warm, bitter taste and is used to flavor or color curry powder, mustard, butter, and cheese. Curry is generally prepared in a sauce. Curry dishes prepared in the southern states of India, where the word also originated, may be spiced with leaves from the curry tree.

Ingredients

  • 1 whole chicken, cut into pieces
  • 1 onion, chopped
  • 1/4 cup vegetable oil
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tablespoon curry powder
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 1 teaspoon white sugar
  • 1 teaspoon ground black pepper
  • 1/2 teaspoon ground cinnamon
  • 1/2 teaspoon ground ginger

Cooking Steps

  1. Heat oil in a skillet over medium heat.
  2. Saute onion until lightly browned.
  3. Stir in garlic, curry powder, salt, sugar, pepper, cinnamon, and ginger.
  4. Continue stirring for 2 minutes.
  5. Add chicken pieces, tomato paste, yogurt, and coconut milk.
  6. Bring to a boil, reduce heat, and simmer for 20 to 25 minutes.
